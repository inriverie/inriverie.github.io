Product Details View

Zappos Mobile

UX & Interface Design

The Zappos Mobile team launched the iPad app when much of the mobile space was still being discovered. As a result, the product detail view was built around utilitarian practices rather than the unique problems new platforms present.

My goal was to realign the design to customer behavior without interfering with them making an informed purchase decision.

Ergonomics

I had users interact with a background image to measure distances and observe comfort levels, particularly for two–handed use.

Concepts & Wireframes

I started by taking inventory of elements then grouped them based on an purchase flow and information seeking flow. Knowing that the layout needed to accommodate thumb input, my concepts focused on keeping interactions at the screen edge.

Final Designs

The vertical space offered in portrait orientation—along with it being the preferred orientation for reading content—lent itself well to the information seeking customer without reducing the image size or impacting the “add to cart” flow.

The consumption of media influenced the decision to make the landscape orientation focus on the imagery while keeping the purchase flow intact. Product information is exposable with a vertical scroll.
